### Understanding Different Types of Sports Bets

  

The community covers various betting types that members should understand:

  

**Moneyline Bets**

These are straight-up picks on which team will win.

No point spreads involved - just pick the winner.

Favorites pay less than underdogs due to lower risk.

  

**Point Spread Betting**

The most common type in American sports betting.

The favorite must win by more than the spread number.

Underdogs can lose by less than the spread and still win the bet.

  

**Over/Under (Totals)**

Betting on the combined score of both teams.

You're wagering whether the total points will be over or under the bookmaker's line.

Popular for games where picking a winner is difficult.

  

**Parlay Bets**

Multiple bets combined into one wager for bigger payouts.

All selections must win for the parlay to cash.

Higher risk but much higher potential rewards.

  

**Proposition Bets**

Specific outcome bets not related to the final score.

Examples: Will a player score over 20 points? How many strikeouts will a pitcher have?

Often featured in the community's daily discussions.

  

**Futures Bets**

Long-term wagers on season outcomes.

Examples: Super Bowl winner, MVP awards, division champions.

Usually available year-round with changing odds.

  

**Live Betting**

Placing bets while games are in progress.

Odds change in real-time based on game flow.

Requires quick decision-making and game knowledge.

  

**Teaser Bets**

Modified parlays that adjust point spreads in your favor.

Lower payouts than regular parlays but higher win probability.

Popular for NFL and NBA betting.

  

The Run With Ricky community provides picks across most of these betting types, helping members diversify their wagering strategies.
